A City Connected

With city’s growing success, the Loop 202 expansion connects the East and West valley. The $1.9 billion project is a commitment to the city’s growth and success. As the 13th largest metro area in the U.S., the city stays connected by public and ground transportation.

A Vibrant Center

With a $5 billion investment, Downtown Phoenix has grown into a destination for people from all over the world. The city center features a vibrant nightlife, dining, and music scene. Historic warehouses and homes have transformed into contemporary restaurants and performance venues.

Cities and Towns

Diverse in Life

The Greater Phoenix Area is made up of more than 20 cities and towns. Behind every corner is a place to call home, each boasting its own culture, history, and attractions. Whether you choose to live in downtown Phoenix or the suburbs of Chandler, you will find a community that embodies your lifestyle. Phoenix sets itself apart from other cities for its qualify of life and innovation.

Film in Phoenix

For more than 50 years, Phoenix has made its name in the film industry as the location for many motion pictures, TV shows, and commercials. Clint Eastwood’s movie “The Gauntlet” and the opening scenes of Alfred Hitchcock’s “Psycho” were filmed in downtown Phoenix. The city’s versatile landscape and sunny weather attract top filmmakers, talent, and crew from all over the world. Arizona’s Reel Savings program provides filmmakers with discounts on hotels, restaurants, rental cars, and equipment vendors as an incentive to call “action” in our city.
